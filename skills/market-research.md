# Market Research Best Practices

## Industry Research Process
1. Identify the niche and its sub-segments.
2. Find industry size data (IBISWorld, Statista, trade associations, Census Bureau).
3. Map the competitive landscape: direct competitors, adjacent tools, manual alternatives.
4. Identify key pain points via forums, Reddit, reviews, support forums.
5. Estimate Total Addressable Market (TAM), Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM), and Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM).

## Competitive Analysis
- For each competitor, document: name, pricing, key features, limitations, target audience, funding.
- Score each on: UX quality, pricing accessibility, feature completeness, market awareness.
- Identify gaps: what do users complain about? What's missing?
- Check review sites: G2, Capterra, TrustRadius, app store reviews.

## Customer Discovery
- Define the ideal customer profile (ICP): company size, role, industry segment.
- Find real companies that match the ICP (LinkedIn, industry directories).
- Document: company name, size, location, likely pain points, decision-maker roles.
- Structure as a Markdown table for easy scanning.

## TAM/SAM/SOM Estimation
- TAM: total market revenue if 100% market share.
- SAM: segment you can realistically reach with your product.
- SOM: realistic capture in first 1-2 years.
- Always cite data sources. If exact data unavailable, state assumptions clearly.
- Use bottom-up estimation: (number of businesses) x (willingness to pay) = market size.

## Channel Research
- For each distribution channel, document:
  - Platform name and URL.
  - Audience size and relevance (1-10).
  - Effort vs. impact score (High/Medium/Low).
  - Entry strategy (how to participate without being spammy).
  - Cost (free, paid, sponsorship).

## Sources
- Always cite sources with URLs or publication names.
- Prefer primary data (government stats, trade associations) over secondary.
- Cross-reference claims across multiple sources.
- Note when data is estimated vs. verified.
