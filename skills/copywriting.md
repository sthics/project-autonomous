# Copywriting & Marketing Best Practices

## Headlines
- Lead with the benefit, not the feature.
- Be specific: "Save 6 hours/week on permit tracking" beats "Better permit management."
- Use numbers and specifics when possible.
- Keep headlines under 10 words.

## Value Proposition
- Formula: [Product] helps [audience] [achieve outcome] by [mechanism].
- Focus on the pain point first, then the solution.
- One value prop per page/email — don't dilute.

## Cold Email
- Subject line: short (5-7 words), curiosity-driven or benefit-driven.
- Opening line: reference something specific about the recipient (recent project, company milestone).
- Body: problem → solution → proof → CTA. Keep under 150 words.
- CTA: one clear ask (reply, book a call, try the demo).
- Always include opt-out link and physical address placeholder (CAN-SPAM/GDPR).

## Landing Page Copy
- Above the fold: headline + subheadline + CTA + hero image/screenshot.
- Problem section: describe the pain in the customer's language.
- Solution section: show how the product solves it (screenshots, demo GIF).
- Social proof: testimonials, customer logos, usage stats.
- FAQ: address top 3-5 objections.
- Final CTA: repeat the primary action.

## Tone for Niche Industries
- Professional but approachable — avoid jargon the audience doesn't use.
- Use the industry's own terminology (they say "work orders" not "tasks").
- Reference specific workflows they'll recognize.
- Don't over-promise; under-promise and over-deliver.

## Compliance
- All outreach drafts marked as DRAFT until human approves.
- Include CAN-SPAM/GDPR compliance elements in every email.
- Only use publicly available contact information.
- No spam tactics, fake urgency, or misleading claims.
